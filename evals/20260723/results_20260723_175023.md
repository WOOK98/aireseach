## 1. INVESTMENT THESIS

We are **Overweight** the humanoid actuator supply chain (precision gears, frameless torque motors, encoders, ball screws, and integrated actuator assemblies) and **Neutral/Underweight** the humanoid OEMs over the next 12–24 months.

The market is pricing the **end-robot narrative** but is underpricing the **physical bottleneck**: actuators represent **30–40% of humanoid BOM** (roughly **$8k–$15k per unit at current pilot volumes**), and long-lead precision components (harmonic/RV reducers, rare-earth torque motors, force sensors) have **6–18 month lead times** with limited qualified supplier capacity. With 2024–2025 marking the shift from one-off prototypes to production-intent pilot programs (Tesla Optimus, Figure 02/03, Agility Digit, 1X, UBTech), the **order inflection will show up in component suppliers first**, driving pricing power and margin leverage for the top tier.

The market is missing that **software generalization can be delayed, but the robot cannot ship without actuators**—and the supply chain is already capacity-constrained.

**Conviction:** *Overweight precision actuators > humanoid OEMs; the parts shortage will outperform the software story over the next two years.*
